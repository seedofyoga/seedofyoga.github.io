## Facebook Post

Yoga means more than posture. The tradition includes breathing practices, meditation, ethical guidelines — and, at a subtler level, specific techniques for directing and containing energy within the body. Two of these are mudras and bandhas.

They tend to receive less attention in modern yoga classes. In classical hatha yoga, however, they are considered an integral part of a complete practice. Mudras — deliberate gestures of the hands, eyes, or body — affect the flow of prana and cultivate specific inner states. Bandhas — internal muscular contractions applied at the root, the abdomen, and the throat — are energetic seals designed to contain and redirect that energy rather than let it dissipate.

Neither is essential for a beginner. But as a practice deepens, they offer a way of engaging with subtler layers of experience. Not just what the body does, but what it feels. Not just the shape of a posture, but the quality of attention brought to it. I wrote an introduction to the main ones — five mudras and four bandhas — with instructions and what each one is designed to do.
