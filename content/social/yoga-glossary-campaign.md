## Facebook Post

You walk into your first yoga class and the teacher starts speaking in a language you don't recognise. _Adho Mukha Svanasana. Ujjayi. Savasana._ You follow along as best you can, but part of your mind starts keeping a list.

Most of us arrive at yoga through the body — the postures, the breathing, the quiet at the end. What we don't always expect is to encounter a whole vocabulary. Sanskrit terms, philosophical concepts, the names of eight limbs of a path most of us never knew existed. Over time, this vocabulary stops feeling like a barrier. It becomes a more precise way of pointing at real experiences.

I wrote a glossary for the terms you are most likely to encounter — on the mat, in texts, and in conversation with other practitioners. The foundations (yoga, prana, chakras), the eight limbs of Patanjali's path, common posture terms, breath and gesture practices, sacred language like Om and mantra, and the key texts and teachers behind the tradition. Not exhaustive. A starting point.
